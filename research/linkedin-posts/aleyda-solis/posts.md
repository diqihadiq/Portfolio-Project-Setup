How to Build a Representative AI Search Prompt Library for Better AI Visibility Measurement.

One of the biggest mistakes I see with AI search tracking is using a non-representative prompt library to assess a brand's AI search visibility. This can give you an inaccurate view of your AI search presence.

A representative AI search prompt library should not be a random list of prompts or a tool's default prompt set. It should be a structured sample of the AI-assisted journeys that matter to your business, covering the right products, audiences, markets, journey stages, competitors and buyer constraints. The goal is to measure the right prompts well enough to identify patterns, diagnose gaps and guide optimization actions.

This guide covers:
- What an AI search prompt library is.
- The biggest mistakes to avoid.
- Why representative doesn't mean exhaustive.
- How to define what the prompt library needs to represent.
- How to build, group and localize prompts.
- How to measure AI search visibility defensibly.
- How to turn prompt findings into optimization actions.
- What a final prompt library can look like.

### Post 4
**Date:** 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_seofomo-activity-7461825726250520577-_Wbb
**Content:**
In practice, I don't really care what optimizing for AI Search is called: SEO, AI Search Optimization, AEO, GEO, or whatever the new acronym ends up being. I'm the first one to adapt to the terminology clients use if it helps them understand the opportunity, get internal buy-in, and move faster.

However, the problem comes when a new label makes AI Search Optimization look like something completely separate from SEO. Because this can backfire in two important ways:

First, it makes it less obvious to decision makers that SEO specialists are the best placed to own and grow this area. SEOs already understand how search systems discover, crawl, render, interpret, evaluate, rank, cite, and drive demand through content, links, entities, authority, technical accessibility, and brand signals.

Second, it makes it easier for tactics to be sold as "new" AI visibility solutions while being disconnected from SEO processes, quality standards, and search fundamentals.

Google's new guidance for optimizing for AI experiences makes it clear: SEO principles still apply in AI Search. Its existing spam policies apply to AI Overviews and AI Mode.

Key points:
- AI Search doesn't replace SEO, it expands where SEO needs to operate.
- Sustainable AI Search visibility won't come from ignoring SEO best practices. It will come from applying them better across your own site and the wider ecosystem where AI systems learn, cite, validate, and recommend information.

### Post 5
**Date:** April 2026
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_i-went-through-9-months-of-semrush-ai-activity-7461023056300507136-1zN-
**Content:**
I went through 9 months of Semrush AI Visibility Index source data comparing ChatGPT and Google AI Mode across Business Services, Consumer Electronics, Software, Fashion and Finance.

The top outcome: the two platforms rely on different source ecosystems, and they're constantly moving.

Key patterns:
1. ChatGPT is much more diverse. In April 2026, ChatGPT needed 1,621 brands to reach 80% share of voice. Google AI Mode needed 299. That is a 5.4x gap.
2. YouTube is far more prominent in Google AI Mode. It became AI Mode's #1 Overall source in April, jumping from 16.0 to 46.9 in Semrush's source score from March to April.
3. Wikipedia has been a major ChatGPT source, but April changed the story. It was ChatGPT's #1 or #2 Overall source from Aug 2025 to Mar 2026, then dropped to #5 in April.
4. Alibaba is one of the most interesting new entrants: zero Google AI Mode top-10 appearances, but became ChatGPT's #4 Overall source in April and #1 in Consumer Electronics.
5. The platforms overlap, but not enough to treat them the same. April top-10 source overlap ranges from 4/10 to 6/10 by vertical. Reddit is the strongest cross-platform constant, appearing in the top 10 for every analysed vertical in both platforms.

The takeaway: AI search visibility is not only about optimizing your own site, but about understanding which external sources each platform repeatedly surfaces, and tracking how that source layer changes over time. One AI visibility strategy for every platform is not enough.
